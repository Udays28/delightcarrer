Top carreer option after 12th from PCB

Doctor

"Studying to become a doctor means dedicating yourself to learning the intricate workings of the human body and mind, striving to alleviate suffering and promote health for individuals and communities."

Doctor

Being a doctor is one of the most respected professions globally, associated with saving and improving lives. As a doctor, you would diagnose and treat illnesses, provide preventive healthcare advice, and sometimes perform surgeries. The journey begins with an MBBS (Bachelor of Medicine, Bachelor of Surgery) degree, followed by a postgraduate specialization in a specific field of medicine, and finally, super-specialization if desired. This could lead to becoming a cardiologist, neurologist, gynecologist, etc. This profession demands a high level of dedication, a strong academic background, good patient interaction skills, and continual learning.
Qualification: MBBS degree followed by specialization through MD or MS.
Average Salary in India: INR 10-18 lakhs per annum.

Dentist

"Studying to become a dentist involves mastering oral health to relieve pain, restore function, and enhance smiles. Dentists prevent and treat oral diseases while promoting good hygiene and educating patients for lifelong wellness."

Dentist

Dentistry is a promising career for those interested in oral healthcare. A dentist's role encompasses diagnosing and treating problems with a patient's teeth, gums, and related parts of the mouth. They provide advice and instructions on oral healthcare, including diet, brushing, flossing, and the use of fluorides. They remove tooth decay, fill cavities, repair fractured teeth, and straighten teeth. To become a dentist, you need to complete a Bachelor of Dental Surgery (BDS Course) degree, and you can further specialize in areas like Orthodontics, Periodontics, or Oral Surgery.
Qualification: Bachelor of Dental Surgery (BDS).
Average Salary in India: INR 3-6 lakhs per annum.

Medical Lab Technician

"Becoming a medical lab technician involves analyzing samples to detect diseases and disorders, providing essential information for diagnoses and treatment. It's about precision testing and ensuring reliable results to support patient care."

Medical Lab Technician

Medical Lab Technicians, often the unsung heroes of healthcare, play a vital role in diagnosing and treating diseases. They perform various tests on patient samples to assist doctors in identifying the exact health condition. These tests range from simple pre-marital blood screenings to more complex tests to uncover diseases such as HIV/AIDS, diabetes, cancer, and many more. To become a Medical Lab Technician, you need a degree or diploma in Medical Laboratory Technology
Qualification: Diploma in Medical Laboratory Technology (DMLT) or B Voc in MLT or B.Sc. in Medical Laboratory Technology (MLT).
Average Salary in India: INR 2-3 lakhs per annum.

Operation Theatre Technician

"Becoming an operation theatre technician involves preparing and maintaining surgical instruments, ensuring a sterile environment, and assisting surgeons during procedures. They play a crucial role in patient safety and the smooth functioning of the operating room."

Operation Theatre Technician

An Operation Theatre Technician prepares the operation theatre before surgeries, sterilizes the equipment, assists surgeons during operations, and takes care of all the work related to the surgical procedure. The responsibilities of OT Technicians are vast, and they must be knowledgeable about all surgical equipment and procedures. They play a significant role in ensuring a safe environment within the theatre and assisting during an operation. A degree or diploma in Operation Theatre Technology can lead to a career in this field.
Qualification: Diploma in Operation Theatre Technology (DOTT) or B Voc in OT Technician or B.Sc. in Operation Theatre Technology.
Average Salary in India: INR 2-4 lakhs per annum.

Pharmacist

"Becoming a pharmacist involves mastering medication therapy, advising on drug interactions, and ensuring safe and effective use of prescriptions. Pharmacists play a vital role in patient health by providing medication management and counseling."

Pharmacist

Pharmacists are experts in medicine and its therapeutic use. As a pharmacist, you would be responsible for providing prescriptions to patients as per a doctor's orders, advising patients about how to take the prescribed medications, potential side effects, and answer any queries they have. Pharmacists also advise doctors and other health professionals on the selection and effects of medications. To become a pharmacist, you would need to complete a Bachelor of Pharmacy (B.Pharm) and register with the state Pharmacy Council.
Qualification: Bachelor of Pharmacy (B.Pharm).
Average Salary in India: INR 2-5 lakhs per annum.

Clinical Researcher

"Pursuing a career as a clinical researcher involves designing and conducting trials to evaluate new treatments and therapies. Clinical researchers are essential in advancing medical knowledge, ensuring the safety and efficacy of new interventions, and ultimately enhancing patient care."

Clinical Researcher

Clinical researchers play a critical role in advancing medical knowledge. They design and conduct studies that investigate both human diseases and methods to prevent and treat them. The work of a clinical researcher might include developing a clinical trial protocol, creating data collection forms, recruiting patients, monitoring the trial's progress, and interpreting the results. A degree in medicine, pharmacy, life sciences, or biotechnology can lead to a career in clinical research. Further specialization is often achieved through a Master's or Ph.D. program.
Qualification: B.Sc. in Clinical Research and Molecular Biology, followed by a Master's degree for better prospects.
Average Salary in India: INR 3-6 lakhs per annum.

Nutritionist/Dietitian

"Training to be a nutritionist or dietitian involves understanding the science of food and its impact on health. They create personalized nutrition plans, promote healthy eating habits, and support individuals in managing health conditions through diet, playing a vital role in overall well-being and disease prevention."

Nutritionist/Dietitian

Nutritionists and dietitians are health professionals who specialize in diet, food, and nutrition. They advise people on what to eat to lead a healthy lifestyle or achieve specific health-related goals. They can work in a variety of settings including hospitals, public health clinics, or sports organizations, and can specialize in areas such as pediatric nutrition or diabetic diets. A Bachelor’s degree in Nutrition and Dietetics is the first step on this career path, often followed by postgraduate study and a period of supervised practical experience.
Qualification: Bachelor of Science (B.Sc.) in Nutrition and Dietetics.
Average Salary in India: INR 3-5 lakhs per annum.

Nurse

"Training to be a nurse involves learning comprehensive patient care, from administering medications to supporting recovery. Nurses are crucial in healthcare, providing compassionate support, monitoring patient conditions, and educating patients and families on health management."

Nurse

Nurses are central to healthcare delivery. They provide care to patients in hospitals, outpatient clinics, and other healthcare facilities. Their duties range from taking and monitoring vital signs, administering medication, assisting in medical procedures, and providing emotional support to patients and their families. To become a registered nurse, you would need to complete a Bachelor of Science in Nursing (BSN) or a related field and pass a national licensing examination.
Qualification: Bachelor of Science (B.Sc.) in Nursing.
Average Salary in India: INR 2-5 lakhs per annum.

Psychologist/Counselor

"Training to be a psychologist or counselor involves mastering the study of human behavior, emotions, and mental processes. They provide therapy and guidance, helping individuals navigate life's challenges and improve their mental health, playing a crucial role in promoting emotional well-being and personal growth."

Psychologist/Counselor

Psychologists study the human mind and behavior, while counselors help people deal with personal issues and mental health disorders. They can work in a wide range of environments, including schools, hospitals, businesses, and government agencies, and can specialize in numerous areas such as clinical psychology, counseling psychology, educational psychology, and organizational psychology. To become a psychologist or counselor, you typically need to complete a Bachelor's degree in Psychology followed by a Master's or Doctorate, depending on the specific career goals.
Qualification: Bachelor's in Psychology, followed by a Master's degree and/or a PG Diploma in counseling.
Average Salary in India: INR 2-5 lakhs per annum.

Biotechnologist

"Becoming a biotechnologist involves delving into the intersection of biology and technology, harnessing living organisms and systems to develop innovative solutions. They play a pivotal role in various fields such as medicine, agriculture, and environmental science, contributing to advancements that improve health, sustainability, and quality of life."

Biotechnologist

t

Biotechnologists work at the intersection of biology and technology, using the tools of modern molecular biology to manipulate the genetic material of living organisms. This field has wide-ranging applications from healthcare to agriculture, with potential roles in pharmaceutical companies, research institutes, and agricultural firms. As a biotechnologist, you might genetically engineer bacteria to produce human insulin, create disease-resistant crops, or develop new biodegradable materials. A degree in biotechnology, or in a related life science field with a specialization in biotechnology, is typically required.
Qualification: B.Sc. in Biotechnology, further studies in M.Sc. and Ph.D. for better job prospects.
Average Salary in India: INR 3-6 lakhs per annum.

Geneticist

"Becoming a geneticist involves unraveling the intricacies of genes and heredity, exploring how they influence traits and diseases. Geneticists conduct research, diagnose genetic disorders, and develop therapies, contributing to our understanding of biology and offering insights into personalized medicine and genetic counseling."

Geneticist

Geneticists study genes, heredity, and variation in organisms. They look at how traits are inherited from one generation to the next and how they vary between individuals and populations. This can involve conducting research into genetic diseases, developing gene therapies, or breeding plants and animals. Geneticists often work in research roles in universities, healthcare settings, or agricultural companies. The field typically requires a degree in genetics or a related discipline and often requires a Ph.D. for advanced research roles.
Qualification: Bachelor's degree in Genetics, followed by a Master's degree or Ph.D. in Genetics.
Average Salary in India: INR 3-6 lakhs per annum.

Microbiologist

"Becoming a microbiologist involves studying the smallest forms of life - bacteria, viruses, fungi, and more - and their impact on health, the environment, and industry. Microbiologists conduct research, analyze microorganisms, and develop solutions for issues ranging from infectious diseases to food safety, playing a crucial role in understanding and harnessing the power of these tiny organisms for the benefit of humanity."

Microbiologist

Microbiologists study the smallest living organisms – bacteria, viruses, fungi, algae, and protozoa. They study the characteristics of these microorganisms, their habitats, and their interactions with plants, animals, and other microbes. Microbiologists can work in a variety of settings, including research institutions, healthcare settings, the food and beverage industry, and environmental organizations. They might conduct research to develop new drugs, monitor and control the spread of diseases, or improve agricultural yields. A degree in microbiology course or a related life science discipline is usually required.
Qualification: B.Sc. in Microbiology, further studies in M.Sc. and Ph.D. for research roles.
Average Salary in India: INR 2-5 lakhs per annum.

Food Technologist

"Becoming a food technologist involves exploring the science behind food production, preservation, and safety. They develop new food products, improve existing ones, and ensure they meet quality standards. Food technologists play a vital role in ensuring food safety, enhancing nutritional value, and meeting consumer preferences, contributing to the innovation and sustainability of the food industry."

Food Technologist

Food technologists ensure the safety and quality of food. They might develop new food products, devise better food processing techniques, or conduct tests to ensure that products comply with food safety regulations. They often work for food manufacturers, in research and development, or quality assurance. As a food technologist, you might create a new recipe for a food product, develop a method to extend the shelf life of a product or check the nutritional content of food products. A degree in food science or a related field is typically required.
Qualification: B.Tech. in Food Technology.
Average Salary in India: INR 3-5 lakhs per annum.

Marine Biologist

"Becoming a marine biologist involves diving into the depths of oceans and seas to study marine life and ecosystems. They research marine organisms, their habitats, and the impact of human activities on marine environments. Marine biologists play a crucial role in conservation efforts, sustainable fisheries management, and understanding the complex dynamics of ocean ecosystems, contributing to the preservation of marine biodiversity and the health of our planet."

Marine Biologist

Marine biology is the study of marine organisms, their behaviors, and their interactions with the environment. As a Marine Biologist, you could work on a broad range of issues, from conservation of marine habitats to the impact of pollutants on marine life. You might find employment in various sectors, including research institutes, environmental consultancies, marine parks, and aquariums. Some marine biologists also work in the field, perhaps on a research ship, exploring the mysteries of the deep sea and discovering new species. A deep interest in marine life, a strong background in biology, and additional specialized study in marine biology are typically required for this career.
Qualification:B.Sc. in Marine Biology, further studies in M.Sc. and Ph.D. for research roles.
Average Salary in India: INR 4-6 lakhs per annum.

Forensic Scientist

"Becoming a forensic scientist involves applying scientific methods to solve crimes and provide evidence for legal proceedings. They analyze physical evidence such as DNA, fingerprints, and substances, using specialized techniques to reconstruct events and identify perpetrators. Forensic scientists play a crucial role in the justice system, helping to ensure the integrity of investigations and bring perpetrators to justice."

Forensic Scientist

A career in forensic science can be extraordinarily varied and rewarding. Forensic scientists apply scientific techniques to collect, preserve, and analyze evidence during the course of an investigation. You might specialize in a specific area like DNA analysis, toxicology, or digital forensics. The information you uncover would then be used to help law enforcement solve crimes. In this field, accuracy and attention to detail are vital, as your findings could be pivotal in a legal case. A strong foundation in chemistry, biology, or physics, coupled with specialized training in forensic science, is usually required.
Qualification: B.Sc. in Forensic Science.
Average Salary in India: INR 3-6 lakhs per annum.

Medical Writer/Science Writer

"Transitioning into a medical or science writer role involves distilling complex scientific information into clear, accessible content for various audiences. Medical writers create materials such as research papers, patient education materials, and regulatory documents, while science writers communicate scientific discoveries and advancements through articles, blogs, and multimedia content. They play a critical role in bridging the gap between scientists and the public, facilitating understanding and engagement with scientific topics and promoting evidence-based knowledge."

Medical Writer/Science Writer

Medical and Science Writers have the vital job of translating complex scientific information into simpler language that can be understood by a wider audience. This can include writing about new research findings, creating educational materials, writing for pharmaceutical companies, or covering medical news stories. Strong writing skills are a must in this career, as well as a good understanding of scientific and medical concepts. Many medical writers have a background in science or medicine, but they may also have additional skills in journalism or communication
Qualification: Bachelor's degree in any science discipline, with strong writing skills; specific courses in medical or science writing beneficial.
Average Salary in India: INR 2-5 lakhs per annum.
