Top carreer option after 12th from Arts

Journalist

Journalist

Journalists play a crucial role in society by keeping people informed about the world around them. They can work in a variety of mediums - print, television, radio, and online. This role involves investigating and reporting on news stories, conducting interviews, and writing articles. Good communication skills, curiosity, and determination are vital in this role. Higher studies in journalism or mass communication can be beneficial.
Qualification:Bachelor's degree in journalism or communication.
Average Salary in India:INR 3-5 lakhs per year

Social Worker

Social Worker

As a social worker, you would have the opportunity to help individuals, families, and communities cope with problems in their everyday lives. They work in a variety of settings, including mental health clinics, schools, hospitals, and private practices. A degree in social work, psychology, or sociology is typically required
Qualification:Bachelor's or master's degree in social work.
Average Salary in India:INR 2-5 lakhs per annum.

Teacher

Teacher

Teachers are essential to society, responsible for educating future generations. They can work in a range of settings and levels, from kindergarten to high school, teaching various subjects. Teaching requires a degree in education, and many teachers also have a degree in the subject they teach.
Qualification:Bachelor's degree in education (B.Ed.) and a post-graduate degree in a specific subject for higher classes.
Average Salary in India: INR 2-9 lakhs per year

Historian

Historian

Historians study and interpret the past. They research a wide variety of sources to find evidence and then analyze this information to create a coherent narrative. Historians may specialize in a specific era, geographical region, or type of history (political, social, cultural, etc.). A career as a historian typically requires an advanced degree in history or a related field.
Qualification:Master's or Ph.D. in history.
Average Salary in India: Rs 3 - 6 lakhs per annum.

Public Relations Officer

Public Relations Officer

Public Relations Officers manage the image and reputation of individuals or brands. They prepare press releases, handle media queries, plan events, and develop public relations strategies. Good communication, writing, and interpersonal skills are vital. A degree in public relations, journalism, communications, or marketing can be beneficial.
Qualification:Bachelor's or master's degree in public relations or communications.
Average Salary in India: 2-6 lakhs INR per year..

Lawyer

Lawyer/h2>

Lawyers represent and provide advice to individuals, businesses, and government agencies on legal issues and disputes. They interpret laws and regulations, prepare legal documents, and argue cases in court. Becoming a lawyer requires a law degree and passing the bar examination in your respective region or country.
Qualification:Bachelor's degree in law (LLB).
Average Salary in India: Rs 6 - 9 lakhs per annum, but can be much higher with experience and specialization.

Photographer

Photographer

Photographers use their technical expertise, creativity, and composition skills to produce and preserve images that tell a story or record an event. They may work in various sectors like journalism, event photography, portrait photography, or commercial photography. Formal education isn't always required, but a good understanding of photographic techniques and equipment is crucial.
Qualification: No specific degree required.
Average Salary in India:Rs 2 - 5 lakhs per annum.

Event Manager

Event Manager

Event managers plan and organize special events like conferences, festivals, large parties, and concerts. They coordinate every detail of events, from the initial brainstorming to the event wrap-up. Strong organizational and people skills are essential. A degree in event management, hospitality, or a related field can be helpful.
Qualification:Degree or diploma in event management or related field.
Average Salary in India:3-7 lakhs INR per year.
