Top carreer option after 12th from PCM

Engineer

"Choosing to be an engineer is embracing the challenge of understanding and shaping the world around us, fueled by innovation and a drive to solve complex problems for the betterment of society. It's a journey where imagination meets precision, creating bridges between what is and what can be, with each design laying the foundation for a brighter future."

Engineer

Civil Engineers plan, design, and oversee construction and maintenance of building structures and infrastructure, such as roads, railways, airports, bridges, harbors, dams, irrigation projects, power plants, and water and sewerage systems.
Mechanical Engineers design, analyze, manufacture, and maintain mechanical systems. They work on all stages of product development, from design and manufacture to installation and final commissioning.
Electrical Engineers deal with the design and application of electrical systems in various contexts, such as electrical circuits, motors, generators, and systems for transmission and distribution of electricity.
Computer Engineers develop and improve the hardware and software aspects of computing. They might work on system software or computer hardware, but the core of their work lies in computing systems and networks.
Aerospace Engineers design aircraft, spacecraft, satellites, and missiles. They also test prototypes to make sure that they function according to design.
Qualification:BE/BTech in the relevant engineering field.
Average Salary in India:3-10 lakhs INR per year.

Architect

"Becoming an architect is embracing the art of shaping spaces that not only shelter but inspire, weaving together form and function to create environments that resonate with human experience and aspiration. It's a journey where creativity meets technical mastery, where every blueprint is a canvas for crafting the landscapes of our collective imagination."

Architect

Architects plan and design houses, factories, office buildings, and other structures. They consult with clients to determine their requirements and create structural plans that are functional, sustainable, and aesthetically pleasing. They often work closely with civil engineers, interior designers, and construction crews throughout the project.
Qualification:BArch.
Average Salary in India:INR 3-6 lakhs per annum.

Statistician

"Diving into the realm of statistics is embarking on a journey of deciphering the language of data, where every number tells a story waiting to be revealed. As a statistician, you wield the power to uncover insights, drive informed decisions, and navigate the uncertainties of our complex world with clarity and precision."

Statistician

Statisticians design surveys, questionnaires, experiments, and opinion polls to collect data. They then interpret that data and use mathematical and statistical analysis to help solve real-world problems in business, engineering, healthcare, or other fields. Statisticians use statistical methods to collect and analyze data and to help solve real-world problems in business, engineering, healthcare, or other fields.
Qualification:Bachelor's or Master's degree in Statistics.
Average Salary in India: 3-7 lakhs INR per year.

Data Analyst

"Embrace the world of data analysis, where every dataset holds the potential to unveil hidden truths and guide strategic decisions. As a data analyst, you navigate the seas of information, transforming raw data into actionable insights that drive innovation and empower businesses to thrive in a data-driven era."

Data Analyst

Data Analysts collect, process, and perform statistical analyses on large datasets. They discover how data can be used to answer questions and solve problems. With the development of computers and technology, they can analyze large datasets quickly and accurately. Their insights can guide business decisions and influence strategy, impacting the growth and profits of a company.
Qualification:Bachelor's or Master's degree in Statistics, Mathematics, Economics, or Computer Science.
Average Salary in India: 4-8 lakhs INR per year.

Software Developer

"Embark on the path of a software developer, where lines of code are the building blocks of innovation, shaping the digital world around us. As a developer, you craft solutions that empower, from sleek applications to robust systems, bridging imagination with functionality to drive progress in the digital age."

Software Developer

Software Developers are the creative minds behind computer programs. They design and write the software that runs on devices like desktop computers, smartphones, and tablets. This can involve anything from creating applications for specific tasks, like word processing or photo editing, to operating systems that run devices. Some developers create custom software for specific applications, while others develop web services, or even networked applications.
Qualification:BE/BTech in Computer Science or related field.
Average Salary in India: 3-10 lakhs INR per year..

Hardware Technician

"Enter the realm of hardware technology as a technician, where circuits and components become the foundation of seamless digital experiences. As a hardware technician, you troubleshoot, repair, and optimize the physical infrastructure that powers our interconnected world, ensuring reliability and performance in every electronic heartbeat."

Hardware Technician

Hardware Technicians are responsible for the installation, maintenance, and repair of computer systems and other technological equipment. They may work in a variety of settings, including private corporations, government agencies, and non-profit organizations. Technicians may also work with a range of systems, including local area networks (LANs), wide area networks (WANs), and intranet systems.
Qualification:Diploma, Certification, or Bachelor's degree in Computer Hardware or related field.
Average Salary in India:2-4 lakhs INR per year.

Information Technology Consultant

"Venture into the realm of Information Technology consulting, where you merge technical expertise with strategic insight to navigate businesses through the digital landscape. As a consultant, you unravel complexities, architecting solutions that optimize operations, enhance security, and propel organizations towards efficiency and innovation in the ever-evolving world of technology."

Information Technology Consultant

IT Consultants, or Technology Consultants, work in partnership with clients, advising them on how to use information technology to meet their business objectives or overcome problems. They work to improve the structure and efficiency of IT systems in various organizations, providing strategic guidance to clients with regard to technology and IT infrastructures, and enabling major business processes through enhancements to IT.
Qualification:BE/BTech in Information Technology or related field.
Average Salary in India:6-10 lakhs INR per year.

Ethical Hacker

"Embark on the noble journey of an ethical hacker, where cybersecurity becomes a force for good. As a guardian of digital integrity, you wield technical prowess to expose vulnerabilities, fortify defenses, and protect against malicious threats. In the ever-evolving landscape of cyber warfare, your mission is to defend networks, preserve privacy, and uphold the integrity of the digital realm."

Ethical Hacker

Also known as a white-hat hacker, an Ethical Hacker is a security professional who applies their hacking skills for defensive purposes on behalf of the owners of information systems. By deliberately probing for vulnerabilities in a system, they can assess the security measures in place and then suggest improvements. Ethical hackers use their skills to improve security by exposing vulnerabilities before malicious hackers (known as black-hat hackers) can detect and exploit them.
Qualification:BE/BTech in Computer Science or Cybersecurity, often with specialized certifications.
Average Salary in India:4-9 lakhs INR per year.

Scientist

" Embark on the illustrious path of a scientist, where curiosity fuels discovery and innovation shapes the future. As a scientist, you unravel the mysteries of the universe, conducting experiments, analyzing data, and pushing the boundaries of human knowledge. With each breakthrough, you illuminate the path towards a deeper understanding of the world and pave the way for progress in every field of study."

Ethical Hacker

A scientist is someone who conducts scientific research to advance knowledge in their area of interest. In the context of PCM (Physics, Chemistry, and Mathematics), a scientist can work in various specialized sectors.
A Physics Scientist, also known as a Physicist, may conduct experiments and studies to understand the nature of the universe, from particles to galaxies.
A Chemistry Scientist or Chemist may work on different chemical combinations and reactions to develop new compounds, materials, or medications.
A Mathematician uses advanced mathematical theories, computational techniques, algorithms, and the latest computer technology to solve economic, scientific, engineering, and business problems.
Qualification:Ph.D. in Physics, Chemistry, or Mathematics.
Average Salary in India: 6-9 lakhs INR per year.

Pilot

"Embark on the exhilarating journey of a pilot, where the boundless sky becomes your canvas and every flight is a testament to skill and precision. As a pilot, you commandeer aircraft through the vast expanse of the atmosphere, navigating through clouds and currents with grace and expertise. With each takeoff and landing, you embody the spirit of adventure, soaring to new heights and connecting the world through the magic of flight."

Pilot

A pilot is a professional that operates the flying controls of an aircraft. Pilots can work in a variety of sectors. Commercial airline pilots transport passengers and cargo, military pilots undertake missions for defense and aid, and other pilots may be involved in rescue operations, firefighting, aerial photography, crop dusting, etc. They are responsible for the safety of the aircraft, passengers, crew, and cargo on board.
Qualification: Pilot's License from a recognized flight school.
Average Salary in India: 20-35 lakhs INR per year.

Air Traffic Controller

"Enter the dynamic world of air traffic control, where precision and vigilance orchestrate the symphony of the skies. As an air traffic controller, you guide aircraft safely through the bustling airspace, managing takeoffs, landings, and trajectories with unwavering focus and expertise. With each communication and decision, you ensure the smooth and efficient flow of air traffic, safeguarding lives and upholding the integrity of the aviation industry."

Air Traffic Controller

Air Traffic Controllers coordinate the movement of aircraft to maintain safe distances between them. They manage the flow of aircraft in and out of the airport airspace, guide pilots during takeoff and landing, and monitor aircraft as they travel through the skies. This is a high-stress job that requires the ability to make quick decisions, a deep understanding of the rules and regulations governing flight, and excellent communication skills.
Qualification: Pilot's License from a recognized flight school.
Average Salary in India: 6-9 lakhs INR per year.

Economist

"Delve into the captivating realm of economics, where the intricate dance of supply, demand, and human behavior shapes the world's markets and policies. As an economist, you analyze data, forecast trends, and unravel the complexities of global economies, guiding governments, businesses, and individuals towards informed decisions and sustainable growth. With each insight, you contribute to shaping a more equitable and prosperous future for society."

Economist

Economists study the production and distribution of resources, goods, and services by collecting and analyzing data, researching trends, and evaluating economic issues. They apply economic analysis to issues in a variety of fields, such as education, health, development, and the environment. Economists might work for corporations to help them understand how the economy will affect their business, or they could work for the government to study and forecast economic trends. In academia, economists might focus on theoretical or empirical research. To become an economist, one typically needs at least a master's degree in economics, though many positions, especially in academia or research, require a Ph.D.
Qualification: Master's degree or Ph.D. in Economics.
Average Salary in India: 7-12 lakhs INR per year.

Research Analyst

"Delve into the captivating realm of economics, where the intricate dance of supply, demand, and human behavior shapes the world's markets and policies. As an economist, you analyze data, forecast trends, and unravel the complexities of global economies, guiding governments, businesses, and individuals towards informed decisions and sustainable growth. With each insight, you contribute to shaping a more equitable and prosperous future for society."

Research Analyst

A Research Analyst is responsible for studying, interpreting, and presenting data related to markets, operations, finance, economics, customers, and other information related to their field of study. Research analysts can work in various sectors, including finance, marketing, business operations, healthcare, and more. The job usually requires strong statistical and analytical skills, as well as the ability to communicate complex information effectively. A bachelor's degree in a related field is typically required, though higher positions may require advanced degrees.
Qualification: Bachelor's or Master's degree in a related field.
Average Salary in India: 3-6 lakhs INR per year.

Pharmacist

"Embark on the vital role of a pharmacist, where expertise in medications becomes a beacon of health and well-being for communities. As a pharmacist, you serve as a trusted guardian of public health, dispensing medications, counseling patients, and ensuring safe and effective use of pharmaceuticals. With each prescription filled and consultation offered, you play a pivotal role in promoting wellness and enhancing the quality of life for individuals and families alike."

Pharmacist

Pharmacists are health professionals who are experts in medications. They dispense prescriptions to patients and offer expertise in the safe use of prescriptions. They also provide advice on health screenings, vaccinations, and oversee the medications given to patients. Many pharmacists work in community pharmacies, but they can also work in hospitals, clinics, for pharmaceutical manufacturers, and even for health insurance companies.
Qualification:BPharm degree.
Average Salary in India: 2-5 lakhs INR per year.

Astronomer

"Embark on the celestial odyssey of an astronomer, where the cosmos becomes your playground and the stars your guiding lights. As an astronomer, you delve into the mysteries of the universe, studying celestial bodies, unraveling cosmic phenomena, and probing the depths of space and time. With each observation and discovery, you contribute to humanity's understanding of the cosmos, igniting curiosity and inspiring wonder about the vastness of our existence."

Astronomer

Astronomers are scientists who study the universe, including the planets, stars, galaxies, and the broader cosmos. They use telescopes and other scientific instruments to observe celestial phenomena and use mathematical models to interpret their observations. Many astronomers work in research or academia, though there are also roles at government agencies (like NASA) and private companies. Becoming an astronomer typically requires a Ph.D. in astronomy, astrophysics, or a related field, though there are some positions available to those with a master's degree.
Qualification:Ph.D. in Astronomy or Astrophysics.
Average Salary in India:6-9 lakhs INR per year.

Defense

"Step into the realm of defense, where dedication and strategy safeguard nations and uphold peace in a complex world. As a defender, you serve on the front lines, protecting borders, preserving freedom, and ensuring security against threats both seen and unseen. With each deployment and operation, you embody the courage and commitment necessary to safeguard the values and integrity of your nation."

Defense

The Defense sector offers a range of technical roles in the Navy, Army, and Air Force. These roles involve the use of advanced technology, engineering skills, and technical knowledge to support the operations of these services. For example, technical roles could involve working on aircraft systems, cybersecurity, weapons systems, communication systems, or other technology used in defense. These roles often require a degree in a related field, like computer science, engineering, or physics. However, some roles might accept vocational training or on-the-job experience instead. Military service also involves basic training and potential for deployment.
Qualification: BE/BTech in the relevant field.
Average Salary in India:Varies greatly, but typically starting from 6 lakhs INR per year.
